Obama Visit: 5,000 To Take Part In Naked Anti-Homosexuality March

Publish date: 2024-04-09

Several Kenyan leaders, including Deputy President William Ruto, have openly stood against homosexuality terming it 'un-African' and against the Bible; now naked protests are coming.

Republican Liberty Party has issued a notice to the police on planned anti-homosexuality protests ahead of US President Barack Obama's July 24 visit to Kenya.

The procession will be held at the Uhuru Park's Freedom Corner at 10am for two days, on July 22 and 23, just a day before Obama jets in.

Vincent Kidala, party leader, says the objective of going nude is for Obama to 'see and understand the difference between a man and woman'.

Early last week, as Tuko.co.ke reported, anti-gay lobby groups, a group of Catholic chruch members and MPs held protests in the city.

White House spokesperson John Earnest confirmed that the president will discuss the rights of gays in Kenya despite the protests.

His Kenyan counterpart, Manoah Esipisu, also said that the government will not stop Barack Obama from addressing any issue as Kenya believes in freedom of speech.
