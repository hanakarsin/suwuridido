Bonchari By-Election: ODM Rejects Black Ballot Boxes, Raises Rigging Concerns

Publish date: 2024-03-23

- The Bonchari by-election will be taking place on Tuesday, May 18

- ODM and Jubilee are the major parties expected to take on each other in the mini-poll

- Claims of voter and election fraud have, however, begun to come up with ODM raising a few concerns ahead of the polls on Tuesday

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) has raised concerns regarding the integrity of the Bonchari by-election slated for Tuesday, May 18.

The party's secretary-general Edwin Sifuna stated they had witnessed a number of suspicious occurrences just a day before the by-elections raising doubts about whether the mini-poll will be free and fair.

Among the disturbing issues is the availing of black ballot boxes, which he said might be used by competitors in enhancing ballot staffing.

"We know that the ballot material must arrive while intact and opened in presence of all agents. There are a lot of rumours going around regarding the integrity of the process. Some of the ballot boxes transporting voting materials are non-transparent...We've been assured the boxes will not find their way into the polling centres," said Sifuna.

This comes a few days after the party accused the police of harassing and intimidating its candidate and the team behind his campaigns.

Led by Sifuna and Kisii governor James Ongwae, the politicians accused the police of being partisan instead of offering security to all the by-election candidates.

"We condemn in the strongest terms possible the misuse of police in the ongoing campaigns for the Bonchari by-election. Police have been throwing teargas canisters at our small town hall meetings and even protecting the Jubilee candidate," Sifuna stated.

ODM has Pavel Oimeke, who will be battling it out with Jubilee's Zebedeo Opore, who held the seat earlier.

Also in the race is Deputy President William Ruto's United Democratic Alliance candidate Oyioka's widow, Teresa Bitutu.

The seat fell vacant following the death of John Oyioka at a Kisumu hospital on the Monday of February 15, after a long illness.
