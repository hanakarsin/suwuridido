Kilifi: William Ruto says AP officer caught on camera fighting over bribe to be punished

Publish date: 2024-08-19

- Ruto said any member of the disciplined force should handle members of the public with civility while discharging their duty

- The DP said police boss Hillary Mutyambai was aware of the issue and had taken up the matter to ensure the officer was charged in court

- The Kwale AP officer identified as Crispus Katana was heard demanding more bribe from the boda boda operators who were transporting charcoal in Marafa area

A police officer based in Magarini, Kilifi county is now the subject of disciplinary action after he was caught on camera brandishing a pistol on boda boda riders.

In a viral video, Crispus Katana was heard demanding more bribe from the motorbike operators who were transporting charcoal to Marafa in Magarini sub-county.

The two civilians, joined by other road users, reminded the officer that they had given him enough money but he could hear none of it.

The commotion escalated into a near fist fight when the furious cop pushed one of the riders to ground attracting more public attention.

Katana drew his pistol while still demanding for more bribe.

The viral video which attracted the attention of Deputy President William Ruto, who had also tweeted it only to pull it down, said the government had ordered the rogue officer be prosecuted.

Tweeps had trolled Ruto for "taking up police roles" before he took down the video tweet.

"There is no room for impunity even during this time when all systems are geared towards dealing with corona pandemic. Citizens must keep vigil as they adhere to the directives of MoH,” he said before hastily deleting the tweet.

In a subsequent tweet, Ruto said the Inspector General of Police Hillary Mutyambai had taken up the matter and would ensure the officer was disciplined.

"The issue of the police officer mishandling locals in Marafa was brought to my attention by Magarini MP Michael Kingi and I promptly informed security officers of the same and the IG has acted. The officer will appear in court. We should be civil and humane in handling the public,"he said.

The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission report released on December 10, 2019, ranked Kenya Police Service as the most corrupt government agency with a 39.6 % score.

The report revealed that victims were likely to encounter corruption and unethical activities in the hands of police officers.

The Ministry of Interior under which police service falls, topped the list of the most corrupt ministries with a score of 47.5%.

Following the viral video, Katana was interdicted and would be arraigned on Wednesday, April 15.
