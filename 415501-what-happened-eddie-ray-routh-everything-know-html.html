Who is Eddie Ray Routh?

Eddie was born on September 30, 1987, in Lancaster, Texas, United States. He is 34 years old as of 2021. The names of his parents are Raymond Ray Routh Sr. and Jodi Routh. Eddie grew up alongside his sister Laura Bess Routh. After completing his high school education, he joined the Marine Corps.

In 2007, he was deployed to a base near Baghdad, where he served as the prison guard and repaired weapons. He was also deployed to the humanitarian mission in Haiti. In July 2011, he was discharged from the Marine Corps after serving for seven years.

Did Eddie Ray Routh have PTSD?

Within a year after being discharged, Eddie's life was falling apart. He had trouble keeping a job and began behaving erratically, having panic attacks and saying crazy, delusional things. He experienced auditory hallucinations and even threatened to kill himself. Alarmed, his family members took away his guns.

Eddie was admitted to the Veterans Administration hospital in Dallas in July 2011, where he was diagnosed with post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). Doctors prescribed a raft of antipsychotic and anti-anxiety drugs, but he declined the treatment offered at the inpatient facility and stopped taking medications after being discharged.

What happened to Eddie Ray Routh?

On February 2, 2013, the world awoke to the sad news that there was a shooting incident near Chalk Mountain in Texas. Surprisingly, Eddie was the perpetrator of the shooting incident that ended the life of Chris Kyle. Kyle had retired from the military, and he was helping the veterans to deal with mental issues.

On the day of the unfortunate incident, Eddie's mother had asked Kyle for help in dealing with her troubled son. Kyle and Littlefield asked Eddie to accompany them to a remote shooting range at the Rough Creek Lodge and Resort, southwest of Dallas. They were armed with an arsenal of pistols, rifles, and ammunition. Upon arrival at the range, he turned on them and shot them.

Kyle and Littlefield never had a chance to defend themselves. Littlefield was struck seven times in the back, shoulder, head, and hand. On the other hand, Kyle was hit six times in the head, shoulder, chest, and right arm. After killing Kyle and Littlefield, Routh drove Kyle's pickup truck back to his home in Lancaster, Texas.

So, why did Eddie Ray Routh kill an American sniper? At that time, Routh was under extreme mental stress and was convinced that Kyle and Littlefield were planning to kill him as they were not talking to him when he was riding in the back seat of the truck on the way to the shooting range. So, he felt bad about it, and this is the reason why he shot them.

When the case was brought before the courts, he admitted to shooting Chris Kyle and his friend 13 times. Eddie's defence attorneys entered a plea of not guilty by reason of insanity. However, the psychologists testified that he was not legally insane but had a paranoid disorder made worse by his drink and cannabis abuse.

Where is Eddie Ray Routh now?

After deliberating on the case, the jury found him guilty of the murders of Chris Kyle and Littlefield and sentenced him to life in prison without the possibility of parole. Is Eddie Ray Routh still alive? He is still alive and is serving his sentence at the Louis C Powledge Unit in Anderson County, Texas.

What is Chris Kyles' wife doing now?

You must be wondering what Kyle's wife, Taya Kyle, is up to since her husband was murdered by Eddie Routh. She is currently working as a contributor for Fox News and lives in Texas. After her husband was killed in 2013, Kyle began giving speeches regarding his U.S. Navy military service and issues faced by veterans across the country.

She became an advocate for widowed women and families who have lost serving family members. Taya published her autobiography titled American Wife: A Memoir of Love, Service, Faith, and Renewal, in which she explains how she dealt with her husband Chris' untimely death. She and Chris were blessed with two kids, Colton Kyle and McKenna Kyle.
