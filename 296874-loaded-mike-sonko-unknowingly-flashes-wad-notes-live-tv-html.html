Loaded Mike Sonko unknowingly flashes wad of notes on live TV

Publish date: 2024-02-07

If there was any iota of doubt that Nairobi governor, Mike Mbuvi Sonko, is a generous man with deep pockets, then those doubts were dusted on the night of Wednesday, January 23.

The flamboyant and sufficiently philanthropic county chief whose bravado and cockiness attracts both hate and admiration in almost equal measure turned up for a popular TV talk show armed with notes, except the money had nothing to do with the interview.

Appearing on Citizen TV's JKL show on Wednesday, Sonko accidentally flashed a bundle of KSh 1,000 notes stashed in the inside pocket of his expensive suit.

The deputy-less governor was trying to explain why his administration is on a 'firing spree' as he stretched his left hand upwards to display a document while pointing at it with the other hand, when he suddenly flashed the hidden cash.

Sonko whose reign at City Hall has been riddled with chaos and theatrics has on a number of occasions demonstrated that money is the least of his problems.

Yes, he is not everyone's cup of tea, but he also commands a formidable support base with followers who love and hold him in high esteem, mostly because of his generosity and empathy towards poor locals and communities.

The governor, despite his perceived arrogance, short fuse and foul mouth, is known to use his millions to help those in desperate need, and such instances are many.

And just to confirm how generous and kind he can be, Sonko was on December 11, 2018 declared the Good Deeds Goodwill ambassador by Israel for his good deeds.

The governor received the special recognition from the Good Deeds Global CEO, Kaynan Rabino, who paid him a visit at City Hall.

Rabino disclosed they had been following the governor's activities via YouTube and were satisfied he was the kind of leader that others should borrow a leaf from.

"You are a leader who should be emulated by many. People should not take your generosity for granted because what you have done so far is very commendable," he said.
