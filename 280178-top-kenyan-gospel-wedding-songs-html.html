Top Kenyan gospel wedding songs

Publish date: 2024-05-28

Gospel wedding songs are comforting, uplifting, and at the same time, bring joyous sounds as one celebrates their big day. The gospel wedding tracks also fill the people at the wedding with energy as they dance to enjoy the day. On that special day, make sure to select some gospel wedding songs that will play to entertain everyone and we guarantee you, the right mood will be set for you and your guests for the whole of that day.

Here we are dedicated to offer you with top Gospel wedding songs in Kenya that have always matched with the ceremonial occasion. The ball still lands on you because it is your day meaning you have to decide which gospel hit feels right for your big day.

1. MOG - Wedding Day

‘Wedding day’ is a song by a disbanded music group, M.O.G that was released in their ‘Girl of My Dream’ album back in 2016. It is among the Kenyan gospel wedding songs that went viral within a short period after its release. ‘Wedding Day’ is a more to rigorous as well as engaging hit that perfectly fits as a grand entrance. The lyrical content of the music group is great and it is a song to go for.

2. Alice Kimanzi - Waambie

‘Waambie’ is a among the beautiful and good gospel wedding songs released in 2017. This song has everything perfect from the flow, beats, vocals, and content. ‘Waambie’ the title of the song translates as ‘tell them’. If you are looking for a wedding gospel track that will serve as a nuptial at your coming soon wedding, then this song fits perfectly. The lyric has a message that prophecies beautiful things to you significant other. Alice Kimanzi said that her friend’s wedding made her to write the wedding song and therefore you can tell that the content is perfect with beautiful declarations.

3. Willy Paul ft Alaine - I Do

Are you looking for latest gospel wedding songs? Well, ‘I Do’ can be a perfect hit track; it was released in 2017. Yes, it left loads of questions pending as well as tongues wagging, but most people fell in love with it; everything is great form the beats to the content. Alaine, a Jamaican female artist has very powerful vocals and they alternate awesomely with that of Willy Paul. This song earned favor and attention from many Kenyans in that it had 300, 000 views on YouTube within days after it was published.

What people debate is whether the banger wedding song is a gospel or a secular song. Willy Paul is a gospel artist while Alaine is a secular artist. This made ‘I Do’ to receive both positive and negative reception. According to us, the song involves God and it declares the love for the newly wedded couple making a conclusion that it is a gospel track. You can play it as a grand entrance song or any part that you feel the song fits.

4. Benachi - Zawadi Nono

Zawadi Nono’ is among the latest Swahili gospel wedding songs released in 2017. The title translates as a huge prize. Benachi dedicated this hit song to his lovely wife and the video is a live recording of their big day. The lyrical content is fit for a wedding day and therefore you can go for it. The beats are cool and friendly and Benachi soulful voice was perfectly captured. It praises the bride and appreciates God the almighty for joining them. This song also reminds the new couple that Christians believe a wedding is meant for one lady and one guy who are in love. God is the one who joins and therefore all glory should be given unto him.

5. Kambua - Umetenda Mema

‘Ametenda Mema’ released this single hit track back in 2013. The song can be placed under the uptempo gospel wedding songs for its cool beats and great content. ‘Umetenda Mema’ is a translation of ‘done great things’. This song fits in perfectly in different ceremonial occasions. As a wedding song, it will remind the new wedded couple that it is because of God’s grace and favor that they are joined as one. The powerful Kambua vocals crown the song for it makes it more friendly for the wedding occasion. In clear words, this Kambua song is uplifting as well as soothing and you won’t regret playing it at your big and special day. Most people prefer to play it as the bride is walking down the aisle.

‘Adamu na Hawa is among the ‘Traditional gospel wedding songs in Kenya to walk down the aisle to. Why traditional? Two souls with unconditional for each other will be united to each other. It is a sweet song that reminds the Christian young couple to change the world in a positive way and it only through believing in God that the bond will last.
