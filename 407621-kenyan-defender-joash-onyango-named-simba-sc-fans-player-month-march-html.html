Kenyan Defender Joash Onyango Named Simba SC Fans Player of the Month for March

Publish date: 2024-02-24

- Joash Onyango’s popularity has grown in recent weeks, thanks to his performances on the pitch

- The defender won player of the month for Simba SC, beating the likes of Manula and Miquissone

- He is set to play an integral part in Simba SC’s quest for the CAF Champions League

Kenyan defender Joash Onyango has been named Simba SC fans’ player of the month for March

The award was launched by Simba SC in February and the former Gor Mahia man becomes the second recipient.

Winger Luis Miquissone won the first award in February.

Onyango’s victory is quite impressive as he beat the likes of Aishi Manula and Miquissone in the votes cast by the fans.

He garnered an impressive 20,288 votes, with Tanzanian Manula coming in second with 4,673 votes. Miquissone managed just 758 votes to finish third.

The Kenyan defender has formed a formidable partnership with Pascal Wawa and together, they have helped Simba to second place on the log.

Following his personal victory, Onyango is set to be awarded Tsh1,000,000 (KSh 47,000).

Onyango will have Kenyans to thank as well as quite a number rallied behind him on Twitter, urging fans to vote ahead of his competitors.

His popularity in the country grew tremendously particularly after his sensational sentiments where he vowed to tame Mo Salah when Kenya faced off with Egypt in the Africa Cup of Nations qualifier.

Meanwhile, Simba are in the hunt for a place in the quarter finals of the CAF Champions League for the first time in their history, and Onyango will hope his good form continues for the long term.

The Tanzanian outfit face off with AS Vita from the Democratic Republic of Congo at the Mkapa Stadium, where they are yet to lose a game in the competition this season.

Simba are top of the group with 10 points

All down to hard work

KCB head coach Zedekiah ‘Zico’ Otieno recently spoke on Onyango’s upward trajectory where he attributed it to the defender’s hard work.

‘Zico’ once coached Onyango at Gor Mahia and he described him as a player who gives his all on the football pitch.
