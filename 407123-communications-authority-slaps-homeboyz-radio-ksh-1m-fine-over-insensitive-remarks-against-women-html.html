Communications Authority of Kenya (CA) has slapped Homeboyz Radio with KSh 1 million fine over alleged insensitive remarks made by its three presenters against women.

The presenters Shaffie Weru, Neville and DJ Joe Mfalme, who have since been sacked, were accused of making derogative remarks against women during their breakfast show The Lift Off on Thursday, March 25.

The communication regulator, during a press statement on Sunday, March 28, directed the station to issue a public apology on two newspapers with nationwide circulation.

"The authority has directed the station to publish a public apology in two newspapers with nationwide circulation and air the same apology on the station during prime time starting from today March 28, for the next five days," said Mercy Wanjau, the authority's director-general.

Accreditation

Additionally, Mercy suspended the station's breakfast show for a period of six months citing a violation of the programming requirements.

Consequently, she directed all presenters in the station to undergo mandatory training on programming code.

"They should undergo training on programming code by the authority, and training/ sensitisation by the National Gender and Equality Commission," she added.

The state agency further directed the station to ensure that its presenters are accredited by the Media Council of Kenya and files evidence of compliance with the authority.

As previously reported, East African Breweries Limited(EABL) had suspended all their advertisements running on the Radio Africa Group station shows involving the trio.
