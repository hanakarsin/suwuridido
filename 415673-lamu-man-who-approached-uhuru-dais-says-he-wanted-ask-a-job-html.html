Lamu Man Who Approached Uhuru on Dais Says He Wanted to Ask for a Job

Publish date: 2024-07-11
  • On May 20, 2021, James Chomba grabbed headlines after walking towards the dais as President Uhuru addressed Lamu residents
  • Chomba said he did not mean any harm or disrespect while approaching the head of state as he only wanted to get a job from him
  • He said he was arrested and locked up in Mokowe Police Station and was released four days later after intense interrogation

The Lamu man who attempted to go at President Uhuru Kenyatta while he was addressing Lamu residents on May 20, 2021, has said he wanted to ask the head of state for a job.

During his Lamu tour a little less than a month ago, Uhuru's security detail was forced to intercept James Kinyua Chomba who attempted to reach out to the president while he was speaking on the podium.

Speaking to the Daily Nation, Chomba narrated the struggles that pushed him to gather courage and take the risk of breaching the president's security.

He said he did not have an invitation to the Lamu event and when it was time for Uhuru to address the locals in the hall he confidently walked into the venue and waited for an opportune time to carry out his mission.

"Everyone was seated apart from me and a few other individuals whom I surmised were security officers. After around 20 minutes of entertainment, the president arrived and the event began,” he recalled.

Walking towards dais

As soon as the head of state arrived in the hall to give his speech, Chomba said he gathered his stamina, got hold of his identification card, and started his stroll towards the podium.

"The aide-de-camp noticed that I was up to something when I was about three metres to where the president stood. He quickly moved from the president’s back and signalled other officers. That is how I was arrested. I tried to shout out my grievances, but the officers whisked me away," he said.

Chomba noted he only wanted to ask for a job from the first in command while also informing him about alleged corruption in Lamu port as he claimed he had been asked for a bribe when he asked for a job in the port.

Arrested

What would follow is a tough four days as he was arrested and locked up at Mokowe Police Station.

His phone was confiscated and was also grilled on what his real intention was.

The 33-year-old apologised to Uhuru for the security scare and maintained he only wanted to seek help from him.

As TUKO.co.ke reported, while giving his speech during the official opening of Lamu Port, Chomba moved close enough to the dais to startle the president.

In the video shared, a surprised Uhuru, who had moved a step to the side when the man appeared, momentarily stopped his speech while his bodyguards swang into action and got hold of the man.
