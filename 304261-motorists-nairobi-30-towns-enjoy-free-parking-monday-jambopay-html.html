Motorists in Nairobi, 30 other towns to enjoy free parking on Monday - JamboPay

Publish date: 2024-03-03

Do you own personal car in Nairobi ?Then count yourself lucky as all motorists will on Monday, April 29 have opportunity to enjoy a one-day free parking courtesy of JamboPay, the firm that operates the digital revenue collection system for Nairobi City county.

Speaking to Tuko.co.ke, JamboPay CEO Danson Muchemi said the offer will be extended to 30 more towns in five different counties where his firm has been contracted to provide similar services.

The said towns include Meru, Embu, Makima, Manyatta, Kitale, Kiminini, Endebess, Chuka, Nkubu, Runyenjes, Kibugu, Siakago, Kiritiri, Ishiara, Gachoka, Ngiiri Karaba, Maua and Makutano among others.

“We will be paying for motorists parking fees on Monday in counties that we serve in the country as part of celebrations of marking 10 years of our existence in the market.“We have also planned a series of activities this coming week among them tree planting and meeting our clients and the general public,” said Muchemi.

Muchemi further says for one to benefit from the offer, motorists will be required to dial the relevant county USSD codes, web and mobile apps to be eligible for the free parking.

JamboPay has been collecting revenue for Nairobi County since April, 2014 and recently extended its contract with City Hall for three more months as it plans to exit the county.

Muchemi said that the firm has also partnered with the Kenya Forest Service (KFS) and Red Cross to plant 1, 000 trees at the Ngong’ Forest as part of the company’s decade long existence.

“I founded this firm in 2009 in a Cyber Café in Nairobi at the age of 24 years and now it employs over 150 people and has a network of 15,000 agents,” said Muchemi.

Story by J. Papanoo,Tuko correspondent, Nairobi county.
