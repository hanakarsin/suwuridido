Russia announces KSh 100 million food aid to Kenya

Publish date: 2024-04-04

- Russian ambassador to Kenya Maksimychev confirmed his government donated KSh 100 million food aid

- The monetary support will be channeled through World Food Programme to the Kenyan government

- Devolution CS Wamalwa recently received a consignment of 200,000 bags of rice donation from China

Kenya has received food aid in form of KSh 100 million from the Russian government in a bid to help avert hunger.

However, the monetary support will be channeled through the UN World Food Programme which will coordinate its distribution to the affected regions in conjunction with the relevant government bodies.

The Russian ambassador to Kenya Dmitry Maksimychev said the support was an expression of the recently held Russia-Africa summit which seeks to foster relations with African countries.

“This support is an expression of solidarity of the Russian people with the people of Kenya. It will contribute to the achievement by Kenya of the Sustainable Development Goals and the Government of Kenya’s Big Four Agenda in food security,” Maksimychev said.

Foreign Affairs Principal Secretary Macharia Kamau welcomed the good gesture which he said was in line with President Uhuru Kenyatta's Big Four agenda on food security.

“Kenya welcomes the cooperation and support of the Russian Federation. And particularly that this support and cooperation is in line with the Big Four priorities of the president targeting food security. This especially welcome given that it came so soon after the Russia-Africa Summit in Sochi, which President Kenyatta attended,” he added.

The government puts the number of those in need of urgent food supply at close to on million in at least 17 counties.

The government has, however, denied cases of Kenyans dying from hunger in the country, maintaining there was enough food in silos.

Recently, Devolution Cabinet Secretary Eugene Wamalwa received a consignment of 200,000 bags of rice donation from China.

Even as the government puts up a brave face in spite of the concerns, Global Hunger Index ranks Kenya at position 77 out of 119 countries in the developing world on food security.

The reports showed one in every three Kenyans (14.5 million) suffer from chronic food insecurity and poor nutrition annually.
