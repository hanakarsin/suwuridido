Makueni: Ex-OCS Sentenced to 4 Years in Jail for Receiving KSh 5000 Bribe

Publish date: 2024-02-20
  • The ex-cop identified as Henry Mutunga was found guilty by Chief Magistrate James Mwaniki for receiving a bribe of KSh 5000
  • Henry Mutuku had demanded the bribe from Mutuku to secure the release of his motorbike confiscated during curfew hours
  • The police officers involved were Sergeant Salim Kautu and Constable Joseph Okombe, and Shem Maragia

A former Officer Commanding Station (OCS) at Mukuyuni Police Station in Kaiti, Makueni county, has been fined KSh 200,000 or serve four years in jail by a Makueni court for abusing his office and receiving a bribe.

The ex-cop identified as Henry Mutunga was found guilty by Chief Magistrate James Mwaniki for receiving a bribe of KSh 5000 from Joseph Kioko Mutuku at Mukuyuni Police station on September 14, 2020.

Enough evidence

KBC reported in his judgement, Mwaniki said enough evidence provided by the prosecutor Sammy Muriuki to prove that the accused illegally arrested Kioko and took the bribe.

The accused had demanded the bribe from Mutuku to secure the release of his motorbike confiscated during curfew hours in September 2020.

The boda boda rider then reported the matter to the Ethics and anti-corruption Commission (EACC) office in Machakos.

Mutuku arrested

The court heard that Mutunga arrested Mutuku when he turned up at the police station to collect the motorbike.

He falsely accused the rider of trying to steal the motorbike from the police yard and for having bhang and detained him.

Mutuku was released after EACC officers handling the matter intervened.

In mitigation, the accused pleaded with the court for leniency, saying he was remorseful and the sole provider of his two young children.

“I take into account the status of the accused as a government employee working with the National Police Service (NPS) and the effect the conviction has on his career,” Mwaniki said.

Mwaniki handed the convict a four-year jail term or an alternative fine of KSh 200 000.

In the same court, the magistrate ordered the other three police officers involved in the abuse of office charge to be arraigned in court to answer charges.

The police officers involved are Sergeant Salim Kautu and Constable Joseph Okombe, and Shem Maragia.

Trafficking bhang

On Thursday, September 30, a couple in Meru county was sentenced to life for trafficking bhang worth KSh 3.09 million.

Meru Law Courts Senior Principal Magistrate Thomas Muraguri handed James Muthiora, alias, Karinta and his wife Fridah Karimi the hefty sentence on Wednesday, September 29.

Muthiora, 59, and Karimi,50, committed the offence in North Imenti in November 2018 and were arrested with other three suspects Timothy Kinoti Kariaci, Joseph Ekeno Ekeno and Jacob Githae Mukunyu.

North Imenti Directorate of Criminal Investigations officer James Githinji led the pursuit that led to the capture of the suspects at Muthiora's house located at Kooja Estate.
