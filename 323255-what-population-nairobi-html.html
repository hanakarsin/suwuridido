What is the population of Nairobi in 2020?

Publish date: 2024-04-18

Nairobi is undoubtedly one of the fastest-growing cities in the continent and the world. Most of these numbers are contributed by new entrants who come to the city in search of greener pastures. It is also estimated that the population of Nairobi will continue to grow in an upward trajectory by the next decade.

It was estimated that Nairobi’s population would be more than 5 million by 2025. However, despite speculation, the 2019 census has nearly crushed the seven-year prediction set in 2018. Other than the number of people, infrastructural facilities such as highways, malls, and connector roads have also increased, and even others expanded.

All these efforts have been in a bid to cater to the rising number of people and ensure there is sufficient space for everyone. What is the population of the capital city after the August census results were released? We have the details of the city’s population, population density, and the constituencies.

The population of Nairobi as of 2020

Just as the population of the country has been on the rise, recently released census results are a testimony that even those of Nairobi and other cities has been increasing. The population of Nairobi County is a true reflection of the rural-urban migration challenge that keeps coming up every decade. So how many people live in Nairobi? Well, the figure, according to KNBS, is 4,397,073 people. Apart from it being the most populated city in Kenya, it is also the most populous county in the country.

The results mean that the population of the country has grown by more than four times since independence. The population as of 1969 was 10.9 million people but has increased to 47.6 million people. Similarly, Nairobi’s population has risen significantly since the first post-independence-Kenya. In 1969, the city’s population was pegged at 509,286 people against the 4.4 million people.

The number of people has also increased from 3,138,369 people in the 2009 census to 4.4 million in 2019, a rise of one million people. Comparing the years from 1969 to date, and in the past decade, there has been a positive trajectory in the population data.

Nairobi population density

To calculate a population density, you take the total population and divide it by the available area. The resultant figure will determine how many people live per one square kilometre. One significant aspect in this year’s census results was the population distribution across the country and in specific areas

The “green city under the sun” is consistently growing. In total, the size of Nairobi is 696 kilometres squared. On the other hand, the number of residents living in the city is about 4.4 million people. After calculating the population density, it brings us to approximately 6,000 people per square kilometre.

Compared to any other county in Kenya, Nairobi has the most ethnically diverse residents. All the tribes recognized in the constitution as part of the Kenyan population reside here. Furthermore, those who live in the city are either working there or doing business. It means the county brings together the largest working population in the country. It also boasts as the highest revenue collecting county in the country, thanks to its large number of multinationals from Africa.

Several organizations both in Africa and across the world, have their headquarters in Kenya’s capital city. Also, some of the county’s most productive and most profitable companies such as Safaricom have their headquarters here. Other well-performing companies situated in Nairobi include KenGen, Kenya Airways, and Kenya Commercial Bank.

Nairobi has not been left behind both in infrastructure development and economically. While the population surge is expected to strain the available resources and result in a state of unrest as witnessed in other developing countries, Nairobi through its administrative authorities has found a way to deal with the issue. For instance, the county has made attempts to reduce the number of motor vehicles entering the central business district on various occasions. It has also initiated measures to use technology in all its payment services, including parking fees and business permits.

Nairobi demographics

Since its foundation, Nairobi has experienced steady growth in the number of people. The 4.1% growth in population makes it one of the highest growing cities in Africa. It is also the second-largest city in the African Great Lakes even though it is among the youngest cities in the region.

However, there is a disadvantage to this growth. Given the rate of growth contributed by increasing rural-to-urban migration and high birth rates, the city’s economy is yet to catch up. For instance, a more significant population of youth in Nairobi is unemployed despite having the necessary qualifications. It is estimated that about 40% of all the residents living in the city cannot afford proper shelter and other basic needs.

Political administrations and divisions

Nairobi county is run by a governor, one senator, a woman representative, members of the county assembly, and members of parliament. The current governor is Mike Mbuvi Sonko, currently not deputized after Igathe’s exit. Johnstone Sakaja is the senator, and the woman representative is Esther Passaris.

Following the 2010 constitution promulgation, districts were abolished opening way to wards. In total, there are 85 wards within 17 constituencies currently identified by the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Board of Kenya. He is a list of Nairobi county constituencies:

  • Westlands constituency hosts Kitisuru, Parklands, Karura, Kangemi, and Mountain View wards.
  • Dagoretti North houses Kilimani, Kawangware, Gatina, Kileleshwa, and Kabiro wards.
  • Dagoretti South has Mutu-ini, Ngand'o, Riruta, Uthiru/Ruthimitu, and Waithaka.
  • Langata hosts Karen, Nairobi West, Ngumo, South C, and Nyayo Highrise.
  • Kibra hosts Laini Saba, Lindi, Makina, Woodley or Kenyatta Golf Course, and Sarang'omb
  • Roysambu consists of Roysambu, Garden Estate, Muthaiga, Ridgeways, Githurai, Kahawa West, Zimmermann, and Kahawa.
  • Kasarani houses Clay City, Mwiki, Kasarani, and Njiru, Ruai
  • Ruaraka consists of Babadogo, Utalii, Mathare North, Lucky Summer, and Korogocho wards.
  • Embakasi South houses Kariobangi North, Dandora Area I, Dandora Area II, Dandora Area III, and Dandora Area IV.
  • Embakasi Central hosts Kayole North, Kayole North Central, Kayole South, and Komarock, Matopeni or Spring Valley.
  • Embakasi East contains Upper Savanna, Lower Savanna, Embakasi, Utawala, and Mihang'o.
  • Embakasi West has Umoja I, Umoja II, Mowlem, and Kariobangi South wards.
  • Makadara has Maringo/ Hamza, Viwandani, Harambee, Makongeni, Mbotela, and Bahati.
  • Kamukunji has Pumwani, Eastleigh North, Eastleigh South, Airbase, and California wards.
  • Starehe has Nairobi Central, Ngara, Pangani, Ziwani, Kariokor, Landimawe, and Nairobi South wards.
  • Mathare houses Hospital, Ngei, Mbatini, Kiamaiko, Mlango Kubwa, and Huruma wards.

These are the constituencies and wards that form the larger Nairobi county. Due to the tight population density, Nairobi has one of the largest county assemblies in the country. Other than the high-end neighbourhoods, most of these wards have a bordering low-class population forming slums.

The census is reflective of the country’s current status in terms of the number of people, their density and distribution. Following its release by the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics, the president announced that the results were pivotal in planning and dividing the national cake. But more outspoken in this year’s data is the population of Nairobi which has grown by more than one million people over the past decade. The county also leads in the population density and number of unemployed youth. However, there are challenges with the availability of resources to cater for the increasing number of residents.
